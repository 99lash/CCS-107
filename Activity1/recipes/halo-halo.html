Instructions

Ingredientss

  1. Prepare the base: In a tall glass, layer half of the crushed ice. Drizzle with half of the evaporated milk.

  2. Add the beans: Layer equal parts of cooked and sweetened red mung beans, white mung beans, and kidney beans on top of the ice cream.

  3. Add the jellies: Layer equal parts of gulaman, sago pearls, and kaong (if using) on top of the beans.

  4. Add the fruits: Layer equal parts of langka, ube halaya, leche flan, and macapuno on top of the jellies.

  5. Scoop ice cream (optional): Add scoops of ube and leche flan ice cream (if using) on top of the fruits.

  6. Top with crunch: Sprinkle generously with pinipig for a satisfying textural contrast.

  7. Drizzle and garnish: Finish with a drizzle of condensed milk and garnish with candied banana slices (optional).

  8. Serve and enjoy!: Use a long spoon to mix everything together before indulging in this delightful and layered dessert!

  • 1 cup crushed ice

  • 1/2 cup sweetened evaporated milk

  • 1/4 cup red mung beans (patong), cooked and sweetened

  • 1/4 cup kidney beans (kacang), cooked and sweetened

  • 1/4 cup gulaman (agar agar), cubed

  • 1/4 cup sago pearls, cooked

  • 1/4 cup langka (jackfruit), cubed

  • 1/4 cup ube halaya (purple yam jam)

  • 1/4 cup leche flan, cubed

  • 1/4 cup macapuno (shredded coconut)

  • Condensed milk for drizzling

  • Candied banana (saba) slices for garnish (optional)

Tips

Additional Notes

  • You can adjust the amount of each ingredient to your preference.

  • Use any other sweetened beans you like, such as chickpeas or black beans.

  • Substitute fruit ingredients with what's available or in season.

  • Canned langka and ube halaya are readily available in Filipino stores.

  • Feel free to get creative and add your own favorite toppings!

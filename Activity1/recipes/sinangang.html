Instructions

Ingredients

  1. Heat the oil in a large wok or pan over medium heat.

  2. Scramble the eggs: Add the beaten eggs to the hot oil and cook, stirring constantly, until just set. Remove from the pan and set aside.

  3. Saut√© aromatics: Add the onion and garlic to the pan and cook until softened and fragrant, about 2 minutes.

  4. Stir in the protein (optional): If using ham or other protein, add it to the pan and cook until lightly browned.

  5. Add vegetables: Add the frozen or fresh mixed vegetables and cook for 2-3 minutes, until heated through..

  6. Incorporate the rice: Add the cooked rice to the pan and break up any clumps with a spatula. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until the rice is heated through and starts to brown slightly.

  7. Season and sauce: Add the soy sauce, fish sauce, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine and cook for another minute, allowing the flavors to meld.

  8. Return the eggs: Add the scrambled eggs back to the pan and mix everything together evenly.

  9. Garnish and serve: Remove from heat, garnish with chopped green onions, and serve immediately.

  • 3 cups cooked and cooled white rice (preferably day-old rice)

  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil

  • 2 eggs, beaten

  • 1 onion, chopped

  • 2 cloves garlic, minced

  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn), frozen or fresh

  • 1/4 cup chopped ham or other protein (optional)

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce

  • 1 tablespoon fish or oyster sauce

  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper (optional)

  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions (for garnish)

Tips

Additional Notes

  • Use day-old rice for best results, as it tends to be less sticky and fries better.

  • Feel free to customize the vegetables with frozen mixed vegetables, chopped bell peppers, or bean sprouts.

  • Drizzle a little sesame oil over the finished rice for extra flavor.

  • To add some spice, include a pinch of red pepper flakes or chopped chili pepper.

  • Use medium-high heat for crispy rice and flavorful browning.

  • Adjust the amount of soy sauce to your taste preference.

  • Store leftover fried rice in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
